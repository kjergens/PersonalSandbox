How To Make A Four-Color Scheme
Mon Feb 29, 2016
Tweet

For your webpage, logo or bedroom, you might be scared your color choices don't pass graphics muster. I say if it feels good do it, but for you perfectionists here are steps to a fail-proof color palette.

We'll start big: with a 4-color scheme. (In a later article we'll cover 2 and 3-color schemes.)

1. Find a color wheel

They fall into two categories: a light wheel and a print wheel. Light wheels use the primary colors red, blue and green. Print wheels use the primary colors red, blue and yellow.

Don't know which you have? Find red on your wheel then follow your finger to the opposite color. If it's green you've got yourself a print wheel. If it's cyan (teal-looking), you've got a light.

Choose a wheel. You can use the light wheel (below) which uses pure red, blue and green as the primary colors.

2. Pick a starting color

That's it! Just point to a color that speaks to you.

3. Pick a color scheme

You're almost done! To get the remaining colors in your scheme, decide on one of 2 color schemes: tetradic (vibrant, contrasting) or analogous (similar, coherent).
