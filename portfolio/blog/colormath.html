Create The Perfect Color Scheme With This Simple Interactive Tool (Part 1: 4-Color Scheme)

Whether for your webpage, your logo, or your bedroom, you might avoid colors for fear they won't "go". I say if it feels good do it, but to double-check your color selections here's a way to get a fail-proof color palette.

We'll start big: with a 4-color scheme. First find yourself a colorwheel. The more hues the better. Then pick a color you like.

You're almost done! To get the rest of the colors, decide on one of 2 color schemes: tetradic (vibrant, contrasting) or analogous (similar, coherent).

Tetradic

To fulfill your tetradic color scheme, grab every color 90 degree increments from your main color until you return back to the main. (So, 90, 180, and 270 degrees from the main color).
