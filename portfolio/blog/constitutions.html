How Old is the U.S. Constitution Compared to Other Countries'? [Infographic]
July 20, 2016
Tweet

For a young country, the United States has a Constitution that could be described as vintage. At 227 years old, it is the longest-running constitution in history.

Some numbers for perspective:

A little over half of sovereign nations enacted their constitutions after 1990. 90% adopted theirs since the beginning of the 20th century. Only 1 (0.06%, the United States) goes by one written in the 1700s.

It makes sense when you consider nations like Italy and Spain became republics relatively recently. And countries like Finland have scrapped old versions and started anew.

Constitutions that have come and gone since the ratification of U.S.'s include those of Prussia, the Soviet Union, Texas (a sovereign nation for 10 years), Yugoslavia, Czechoslovakia, and East Germany.
