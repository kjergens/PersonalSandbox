Brits, Bugs and Nobility: The Colorful History of Red
Mar 13, 2016
Tweet

The story of red clothes starts with a bug in Mexico.

By the 15th century, Spain had colonized much of Mexico and South America. There they were introduced to these cactus-dwelling bugs and the rich red dyes they produced.

They sold it to English nobility, who salivated over the color. Britain particularly was keen to use it for royal robes and military jackets.

Britain and Spain were political rivals and, while Brits were desperate for Spanish red dye, it pained Queen Elizabeth to send British money to Spain. To combat this she declared it illegal for everyone except the nobility to wear red clothes.

For centries, Spain kept secret where they got this powder and what it was made of.
