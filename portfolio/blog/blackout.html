The Importance of a Creative Blackout Hour

When creativity strikes it's important to capture it, fast! Peel off to the side of the road; wake up, turn the lights on and find a pen; whip out your smartphone Notes app in the middle of a movie -- whatever it takes to trap that idea before it flies away.

But equally important is knowing when to let it go. Working on a project brings up an onslaught of thoughts, with various degrees of helpfulness. "If I change a font here all the fonts have to change." "Should I never have used this font?" "Can I make a font?" "Am I taking time away from illustrating?" "What about the project I wanted to do before this one?" "I need to redo the navigation." "I need to redo all navigations from all my projects." It gets oppressive.

At this point the creative process becomes a Worry Fest and an undoable chore list. Which is when you must allow yourself to let all those thoughts go. Don't write them down. Stop them from breeding.

This is a difficult sacrifice to make. You risk losing a good idea! Many of us have had our best ideas after a trail of hopeless ones.

But if you never shut off that valve your brain will not have room for fresh ideas. Even the best soil needs time off to recover. Your creative mind deserves no less.
