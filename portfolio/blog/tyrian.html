The Most Expensive Dye in the World
Tweet

Royal purple - also known as Tyrian purple after the city Tyre - holds the record as the world's most expensive dye. The story starts with snails. Actually, it starts with snail mucus.

Starting as far back as 500 B.C., people caught snails from the Mediterranean Sea, extracted their mucus, squeezed it into a basin, left it to dry in the sun, watch the color transform from clear to purple, added salt-water and dyed clothes in it.

It was a slow and expensive process. It took 12,000 snails to yeild one gram of dye - enough to dye one handkerchief.

The ordeal made Tyrian purple accessible only to the very rich. Some accounts say it was worth its weight in silver. Others say it was more precious than gold.

It wasn't so much the color of Tyrian purple - a rusty reddish purple that resembles dried blood - that people valued. It was the quality. Tyrian-dyed garments had a luster that got richer and brighter with weathering and sunlight, rather than fading like lower quality dyes.

Tyrian purple remained the color of royalty for centuries. Today authentic snail-made Tyrian purple is hard to come by, but its legacy lasts and purple remains the symbol of royalty.
