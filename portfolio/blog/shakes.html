Think Shakespeare's Bedroom Was Drab? Think Again.
Thu Feb 11, 2016
Tweet

Ok, so you might not have been thinking about Shakespeare's bedroom. But now that you are, chances are you are picturing shades of brown, black, and gray. Because it was the 16th century and people in the past lived without colors, right?

Until recently, that's what the curators of Shakespeare's birthplace in Stratford-upon-Avon thought. They decorated the tourist destination with white walls, gray linens, and bare wooden bedposts.

In 2000, they discovered that most middle-class families of the day, including Shakespeare's, decorated their homes with painted cloths and vibrant linens. Colors were a sign a wealth, and anyone who could afford it showed them off. His birthplace and adult homes have been redecorated in the rich colors that probably surrounded him all his life. Lucky man!
